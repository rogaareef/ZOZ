متى تستخدم أي أسلوب؟

  • APA → علم النفس، التربية، العلوم الاجتماعية
  • Chicago NB → التاريخ، الآداب، الفلسفة

Correct source documentation is not mere academic formality — it is the foundational pillar of academic integrity. It proves to your readers, reviewers, and academic committees that your conclusions are built on verifiable, reliable scholarly foundations. A citation error, however small, can undermine the credibility of even the most brilliant thesis.

Why Citation Style Varies by Discipline

Each academic discipline developed citation conventions that reflect its priorities and the nature of its sources. Social sciences emphasize the publication date prominently (APA) because recency of research matters greatly in these fields. Law prioritizes the hierarchical authority of sources (OSCOLA) because precedent and statutory hierarchy are legally operative. Humanities prefer detailed footnotes (Chicago) to support nuanced scholarly conversation without interrupting the flow of argument.

APA 7th Edition — The Most Widely Used Format

The American Psychological Association's format dominates psychology, education, social sciences, and business research. The 7th edition introduced significant changes including simplified author formatting and new digital source guidelines.

Books and Monographs

Smith, J. A., & Brown, L. K. (2022). Research Methods in Social Sciences (3rd ed.). Academic Publishing House. https://doi.org/10.xxxxx

Journal Articles

Al-Rashidi, M. (2023). Impact of AI on academic writing in Arab universities. Journal of Educational Research, 45(3), 112–134. https://doi.org/10.xxxxx

Websites and Online Sources

World Health Organization. (2024, March 15). Digital health strategies in developing countries. https://www.who.int/news/item/15-03-2024

Key Changes in APA 7th Edition

  • Up to 20 authors listed before using ellipsis (previously only 6)
  • DOI formatted as hyperlink (https://doi.org/...)
  • "Retrieved from" no longer required for most online sources
  • Publisher location no longer required for books

Chicago Style — 17th Edition

Chicago offers two distinct systems: Notes-Bibliography (NB) for humanities, and Author-Date (AD) for sciences and social sciences.

In the NB system, sources appear as numbered footnotes with full citation details on first reference and shortened form on subsequent references, plus a final bibliography. Example footnote: Smith, Research Methods, 47–52.

OSCOLA — The British Legal Citation Standard

The Oxford University Standard for Citation of Legal Authorities differs fundamentally from social science formats. There is no final reference list — all citations appear in footnotes. Sources follow a strict hierarchy of authority.

Legislation: Companies Act 2006, s 172(1).

Cases: Donoghue v Stevenson [1932] AC 562 (HL) 580.

Journal Articles: HLA Hart, 'Positivism and the Separation of Law and Morals' (1958) 71 HLR 593.

MLA Format — For Literature and Humanities

The Modern Language Association format is standard in comparative literature, linguistics, and humanities. It uses parenthetical author-page citations in text and a "Works Cited" list at the end.

Example: Smith, John A. Research Methods in Modern Literature. Academic Press, 2022.

When to Use Which Style

  • APA → Psychology, Education, Social Sciences, Business, Nursing
  • Chicago NB → History, Art History, Philosophy, Religious Studies
  • Chicago AD → Natural Sciences, Social Sciences (alternative to APA)
  • MLA → Comparative Literature, Linguistics, Cultural Studies
  • OSCOLA → British Law, Commonwealth Law, International Law
  • Bluebook → American Law

Most Common Citation Errors

The errors that most frequently cause thesis rejections or point deductions: omitting DOI numbers, incorrect author name ordering, mixing edition formatting rules, failing to distinguish between journal article and book chapter format, and inconsistent formatting across the reference list.
